Jenna Gieszler's Guide to Building a Personal Code of Ethics as a Journalist

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to write a personal code of ethics as a journalism student

Assembly time: Varies between person, but expect a couple of hours to research and develop
Awesome rating: 100% awesome, professional, and lifelong

Are you a jounralism student? Are you looking to have a career in journalism? One of the most important things as a journalist is to understand ethics, and be able to have your own. Follow along with this easy guide to build your personal/professional codes of ethics that you can use now and in the future!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Paper
  • Pen or Pencil
  • Internet Access
  • A Working Document
  • Access to AP Stylebook
  • Most Importantly... Your Brain!

  1. Let's define what ethics in journalism means

    Having a solid code of ethics is what can make or break you as a jouranlist. You can use your personal code throughout your entire career. So what does ethics in journalsim mean? Reporters are guided by a common set of standards known as journalistic ethics. They outline the goals and responsibilities that journalists, editors, and other professionals in the field should adhere to in order to carry out their work ethically. Ethical journalism entails taking responsibility for your work, and being able to justify your reasoning. Watch this short clip below that provides an understaning of ethical journalism!

  2. Research some examples

    Lucky for you there are guidlines you can refer to. It's important that you get your information from well researched publications. The Society of Professional Journalists (SPJ), is a great place to start. They have 4 key practices you should always follow as a journalist. They are: Seek truth and report it, minimize harm, act independently, and be accountale and transparent (SPJ, 2014).

  3. Time to brain storm!

    Think of your personal traits, morals, and values in a personal and professional setting. What makes you, you?

    Image of a person thinking about all their traits.

  4. Write them down!

    Now that you have had sometime to learn and brainstorm, write down as many ethical codes as you possible can.

    Image of writing on a paper.

  5. Formulate your 10 best personal code of ethics

    After some researching and brainstorming you should formulate a solid list of ethical codes you can use in your professional journalism career.

  6. Personal codes of ethics will stick with you.

    It's important to refer to your ethical codes often. These will help you defend your reasoning, and maintain integrity and credibility throughout your entire career.
